Specialized Single-Chassis Window AC Troubleshooting

Unlike split systems, a Window Air Conditioner packages the evaporator coil, heavy compressor, condenser, fan motor, and control board into one structural metal sleeve. This single-chassis layout means vibration, heat, and dirt are concentrated, which can lead to motor bearing failures, capacitor failures, or cabinet rust leakages.

At **Jyoti AC Repair & Service**, we provide certified **Window AC repair and diagnostic** services. Our doorstep engineers carry specialized pull-out rails, frame braces, starter capacitors, heavy-duty fan motors, and brazing units to troubleshoot your window unit safely without damaging your home walls or windows.

Our Complete Window AC Repair Process

We ensure a safe and durable repair through a rigorous 4-step workflow:

1

Sleeve Unmounting & Inspection

Our technicians safely slide the AC chassis out of its wall or window mounting frame, checking the cabinet structure for dirt or rust.

2

Fan Motor & Bearing Audit

We clean the dual-shaft fan motor, check the bearings, and lubricate the motor to eliminate rattling and squealing noises.

3

Compressor Relay & Capacitor Swapping

Using genuine manufacturer replacement parts, we replace failed starter capacitors or contactor relays to restore the compressor's cooling cycle.

4

Angle Calibration & Re-Mounting

We re-mount the unit into its window sleeve, calibrating a slight backward angle to ensure condensate water drains safely outdoors.

FAQs — Window AC Repair in Vadodara

Get instant answers regarding window air conditioner diagnostics and repairs.

Why is my Window AC dripping water inside the room?

This is almost always caused by a **tilted chassis frame or a blocked outer drain hole**. Window ACs must be installed with a slight backward tilt (about 5 degrees) so that condensation drains out the back. If the tilt is misaligned or the back drain hole is blocked by mud, water will overflow into your room.

What causes the loud rattling and vibrating noise in Window ACs?

Loud cabinet noise is typically caused by four issues: loose mounting frame screws, an unbalanced fan blower wheel, worn-out motor bearings, or loose rubber mounting pads beneath the compressor. Our technicians can isolate and resolve these noises within 30 minutes of doorstep arrival.

Do you need to take my Window AC to your lab for repair?

In 90% of cases, we can complete the repair directly at your doorstep. We slide the chassis out of its sleeve to replace capacitors, relays, fan motors, or fill gas on-site. We only collect the unit for our lab if it requires complex welding or major compressor replacements.

Can I upgrade my old window AC remote controller?

Yes. If your old window unit lacks a working remote or the buttons on the front panel are broken, we can install original manufacturer panels or set up premium multi-brand universal remotes to restore convenient cooling control.
